# Schedule Builder Overrides

The schedule builder is the execution surface. It should feel tighter, more utilitarian, and more state-driven than the dashboard.

## Layout

- Use the full available width.
- Prioritize the schedule grid, editor sheet, and decision controls over decorative framing.
- Keep controls close to the grid so edits feel direct.

## Visual Tone

- Reuse the same color and typography system, but reduce atmospheric effects.
- The grid, time blocks, and editing surfaces need stronger contrast than the dashboard widgets.
- Serif usage should be limited to high-value totals or section headers, not every editable area.

## State Language

- Baseline, proposed, replacement, and removed meetings need a stable visual vocabulary.
- Selection should combine fill, border, and text treatment rather than relying on one attribute.
- If a meeting block represents risk or conflict, surface it with label plus color, not color alone.

## Interaction Rules

- Drag, resize, and selection states must be obvious before motion begins.
- Empty areas in the schedule should remain visually quiet so active meetings stand out.
- Editing sheets should read like operational forms, not modal marketing cards.

## Avoid

- Heavy glow, neon, or entertainment-style styling
- Large decorative hero treatment inside the planning workspace
- Ambiguous color states between informational, warning, and destructive actions
